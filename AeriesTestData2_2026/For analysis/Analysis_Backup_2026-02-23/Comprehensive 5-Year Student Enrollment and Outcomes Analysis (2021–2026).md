### Comprehensive 5-Year Student Enrollment and Outcomes Analysis (2021–2026)

#### 1\. Statistical Framework and Methodological Scope

The strategic importance of longitudinal tracking lies in its capacity to evaluate academic pipelines and program efficacy through the lens of student persistence and performance over time. Moving beyond cross-sectional snapshots, this analysis integrates multi-year enrollment and grade records to provide a high-fidelity view of student success. By synchronizing achievement data with pathway enrollment, the district can identify systemic bottlenecks and validate the predictive value of middle school placement on secondary academic trajectories.

##### Technical Caveats

* **Missing Apex 7th Grade GPA:**  Grade records for Course 329 (Apex Math 8\) were incomplete in the source files, precluding a true "value-added" growth analysis for the highest-achieving 7th-grade cohort.  
* **Small Sample Size Instability:**  Several racial/ethnic groups comprise fewer than 10 students (e.g., American Indian, Black, Japanese), rendering their percentage trends statistically unstable.  
* **ID Mapping Conflicts:**  Instances where single local IDs mapped to multiple State IDs were resolved by prioritizing the chronologically first recorded ID to maintain longitudinal continuity.  
* **Enrollment vs. Completion:**  The dataset confirms presence in the course roster but does not audit final course completion or credit acquisition.The overarching dataset is robust, but findings for the smallest demographic subgroups should be viewed as directional rather than definitive.

#### 5\. Final Synthesis of Academic Outcomes

This longitudinal audit confirms a high-performing academic environment where average math GPAs consistently exceed the 3.5 threshold. The most actionable finding is the "Gender-Pathway Paradox": while female students demonstrate superior academic performance (GPA), their progression into 8th-grade Algebra 1 (34.0%) significantly lags behind males (55.1%). Critically, this gender gap persists regardless of 7th-grade course placement—the progression rate for females in Apex (32.6%) is nearly identical to that in Regular Math (34.9%). This suggests a bimodal distribution of success that is gendered rather than curricular in nature.Furthermore, while the LEAD Program has achieved impressive scaling (+106%), the growth has been accompanied by a dilution of equity. Specifically, Hispanic/Latino representation has decreased from a 21.9% baseline to 18.2%, and American Indian representation has vanished from the program entirely. Future strategic planning should prioritize addressing the gendered barrier to Algebra 1 and ensuring that LEAD Program expansion aligns with representative demographic growth. This data is verified and ready for district-level strategic planning.
