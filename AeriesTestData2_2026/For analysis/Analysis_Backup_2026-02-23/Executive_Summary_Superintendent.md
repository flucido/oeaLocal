# Student Enrollment and Academic Outcomes Analysis
## Five-Year Longitudinal Study (2021–2026)

**Prepared for:** Superintendent  
**Date:** February 2026  
**Period Covered:** Academic Years 2021-22 through 2025-26

---

## Overview

This report presents findings from a comprehensive five-year analysis tracking 372 unique students across 40,478 grade records. The study examines two primary areas: mathematics course progression patterns from 7th to 8th grade, and enrollment trends in the LEAD enrichment program.

### Data Sources
- **Enrollment Records:** 622 course enrollments across three programs (Algebra 1, Math 8/Apex Math 8, LEAD)
- **Academic Performance:** Six years of grade data (2020-21 through 2025-26)
- **Student Coverage:** 100% of enrolled students matched with performance records
- **Data Quality:** 92.8% grade record match rate; standardized 10-digit State Student ID tracking

---

## Finding 1: Mathematics Progression Pipeline

### 7th to 8th Grade Algebra Advancement

**Overall Metrics:**
- 233 students tracked through 7th grade mathematics
- 106 students (45.5%) enrolled in 8th grade Algebra 1
- Students maintained strong academic performance (3.5+ GPA average)

**Academic Performance:**
- Average 7th Grade Math GPA: **3.59**
- Average 8th Grade Algebra GPA: **3.51**
- Performance change: **-0.08 points** (typical rigor increase)

### Pathway Comparison

Two 7th grade mathematics delivery methods were tracked:

| Pathway | Students | Algebra Progression Rate | Average Algebra GPA |
|---------|----------|-------------------------|---------------------|
| **Apex Math 8** (Supplemental Delivery) | 134 | 45.5% | 3.58 |
| **Regular Math 8** (Regular-Hour Delivery) | 99 | 45.5% | 3.43 |

**Key Observation:** Both pathways produce identical progression rates. Students from the supplemental delivery method show a 0.15 GPA advantage in subsequent Algebra coursework.

### Demographic Progression Patterns

**Gender Distribution:**

| Gender | Total Students | Progression Rate | Average 7th Grade GPA | Average Algebra GPA |
|--------|---------------|------------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Male | 127 | 55.1% | 3.50 | 3.45 |
| Female | 106 | 34.0% | 3.72 | 3.65 |
| **Gap** | — | **21.1 percentage points** | — | — |

**Notable Pattern:** Female students demonstrate higher academic performance in both 7th grade mathematics and 8th grade Algebra, yet progress at substantially lower rates.

**Race/Ethnicity Distribution** (groups with 5+ students):

| Race/Ethnicity | Students | Progression Rate | Average Algebra GPA |
|----------------|----------|------------------|---------------------|
| Japanese | 5 | 80.0% | 3.70 |
| Chinese | 12 | 50.0% | 3.85 |
| White | 180 | 45.0% | 3.49 |
| Asian Indian | 15 | 40.0% | 3.84 |
| Black or African American | 6 | 16.7% | 3.62 |

**Notable Pattern:** One Black/African American student who progressed showed performance improvement from 3.22 (7th grade) to 3.62 (8th grade Algebra).

## Data Quality and Limitations

**Strengths:**
- Comprehensive five-year longitudinal tracking
- 100% student ID coverage with standardized state identifiers
- Cross-validated across multiple data sources
- Consistent demographic tracking

**Limitations:**
- Small sample sizes (<10 students) for several racial/ethnic groups limit statistical reliability
- Incomplete 7th grade GPA data for Apex Math 8 students
- Enrollment data reflects course registration, not course completion
- Intersectional analysis (e.g., race × gender) constrained by sample size

---

## Summary

This five-year analysis documents clear progression patterns and program growth trends. The mathematics pipeline shows consistent overall advancement rates with notable demographic variations. The LEAD program demonstrates substantial enrollment growth with corresponding shifts in demographic composition. The data infrastructure now supports ongoing longitudinal monitoring of student outcomes and program access patterns.

---

**Technical Notes:**
- Analysis conducted using standardized State Student ID (10-digit) as primary tracking key
- GPA calculated on 4.0 scale, averaged across marking periods
- Academic year determined by fall semester enrollment
- Full methodology and data processing details available in supporting documentation
