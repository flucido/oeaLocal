## 1. Accessing Metabase

### URL
- **Production**: http://localhost:3000 *(update with production URL)*
- **Login**: Use your district email address and password

### First-Time Login
1. Navigate to the Metabase URL
2. Enter your email and temporary password (provided by IT)
3. You will be prompted to create a new password
4. Password requirements: Minimum 8 characters, include uppercase, lowercase, and numbers

### Troubleshooting Access
- **Forgot Password**: Click "Forgot password?" on login page
- **Account Locked**: Contact helpdesk at analytics@district.edu
- **Browser Issues**: Use Chrome, Firefox, or Safari (latest versions)

## 2. Dashboard Navigation

### Finding Your Dashboards
1. After logging in, you'll see the main **Home** page
2. Your assigned dashboards appear in the **"Pick up where you left off"** section
3. Use the **Collections** menu (left sidebar) to browse all available dashboards
4. Use the **Search** bar (top right) to find specific dashboards

### Dashboard Layout
- **Title**: Dashboard name at top
- **Filters**: Filter controls below title (School, Grade, Date Range, etc.)
- **Visualizations**: Charts, tables, and metrics in grid layout
- **Actions**: Download, share, and refresh buttons (top right)

---

## 3. Filtering Data

### Available Filters (varies by dashboard)

**School Filter**
- Select your school from dropdown
- Choose "All Schools" to see district-wide data
- *Note*: Some roles are restricted to their assigned school only

**Grade Level Filter**
- Check boxes for grades you want to see: 9, 10, 11, 12
- Select "All Grades" to see all high school students
- Default: All grades selected

**Date Range Filter**
- Options: Last 30 days, Last 60 days, Last 90 days
- Default: Last 30 days
- Changes date window for trends and calculations

**Risk Level Filter** (Dashboard 1 & 2)
- Low: Students performing well
- Medium: Students showing early warning signs
- High: Students at significant risk
- Critical: Students requiring immediate intervention

### Applying Filters
1. Click on a filter control at top of dashboard
2. Make your selection(s)
3. Click **"Apply"** or dashboard updates automatically
4. To clear filters, click **"Reset"** button

---

## 4. Exporting Data

### Export to CSV (Spreadsheet)
1. Hover over any table or chart
2. Click the **three dots** (⋯) in top right corner of visualization
3. Select **"Download results"** → **".csv"**
4. File downloads to your default Downloads folder
5. Open in Excel or Google Sheets

### Export to PDF (Report)
1. Click **"⋯"** button (top right of dashboard)
2. Select **"Print"** or **"Download as PDF"**
3. Configure page size and orientation if needed
4. Click **"Print"** / **"Download"**

### Print Dashboard
1. Click **"⋯"** button (top right of dashboard)
2. Select **"Print"**
3. Use browser print dialog
4. Tip: Use "Landscape" orientation for wide dashboards

### What You Can Export
- **Tables**: Full data behind any table visualization
- **Charts**: Underlying data, not the visual (export as image separately)
- **Entire Dashboard**: As PDF for presentations
- **Query Results**: From SQL editor (admin only)

## 5. Understanding Dashboard Metrics

### Common Terms

**Chronic Absenteeism**
- Missing 10% or more of school days (18+ days per year)
- National metric used for accountability

**Risk Score**
- Scale: 0-100 (higher = more at risk)
- Combines attendance, behavior, and academic factors
- Used to prioritize interventions

**Wellbeing Risk Level**
- **Low**: On track, minimal concerns
- **Medium**: Early warning signs, monitor closely
- **High**: Multiple risk factors, intervention recommended
- **Critical**: Immediate action required

**Attendance Rate (30d)**
- Percentage of days attended in last 30 calendar days
- Calculation: (Days Present / Total School Days) × 100%
- Example: 90% = attended 27 of 30 days

**Pass Rate**
- Percentage of students with grade C or better
- Calculated per class section or course

**Equity Gap**
- Difference in outcomes between demographic subgroups
- Example: If Hispanic students have 75% attendance and district average is 85%, gap is 10 percentage points

## 6. Tips for Effective Use

### Best Practices
- **Check dashboards weekly**: Monday mornings recommended
- **Apply filters before exporting**: Narrow to your scope (school, grade)
- **Bookmark frequently used dashboards**: Use browser bookmarks
- **Refresh if data looks stale**: Click refresh icon (🔄) top right
- **Take screenshots for meetings**: Use screenshot tool + crop

### What NOT to Do
- ❌ Don't share login credentials with others
- ❌ Don't export data containing student names to unsecured locations
- ❌ Don't modify queries (unless you're an admin)
- ❌ Don't ignore FERPA requirements when sharing data

### Data Privacy Reminders
- **FERPA Protected**: All student data is confidential
- **Role-Based Access**: You only see data relevant to your role
- **Secure Sharing**: Use district-approved methods only
- **Minimum Cell Size**: Cells with <10 students may be suppressed for privacy
