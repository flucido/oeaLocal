## Overview

As a teacher, you have access to one dashboard that helps you:
- ✅ Compare your class performance to school and district averages
- ✅ Identify factors that contribute to student success
- ✅ See which teaching practices correlate with better outcomes
- ✅ Reflect on your own effectiveness using objective data

**Your Dashboard Access**:
- **Dashboard 4**: Class Effectiveness Comparison (filtered to **YOUR classes only**)

**Data Scope**: You see **only your own class sections**. You cannot see other teachers' data.

**Important**: This dashboard is for **professional reflection and growth**, not evaluation. Your principal may use similar data as **one input** in your evaluation, but this specific dashboard is for your personal use.

## Section 1: Your Dashboard

### Dashboard 4: Class Effectiveness Comparison

**Purpose**: Understand how your classes are performing and identify opportunities for improvement

**What You'll See**:

1. **Your Class Pass Rates** (Bar Chart)
   - Shows percentage of students passing (C or better) in each of your class sections
   - Compares to: School average, District average
   - **What to look for**: Classes significantly below average (>10 percentage points)

2. **Attendance Impact on Pass Rate** (Scatter Plot)
   - Each dot = one of your class periods
   - X-axis: Average student attendance rate in class
   - Y-axis: Pass rate (% with C or better)
   - **What it shows**: Strong attendance usually correlates with higher pass rates
   - **Insight**: If you have high attendance but low pass rate, may indicate instructional challenge

3. **Your Effectiveness Indicators** (4 Metric Cards)
   - **Pass Rate**: Overall % of your students passing
   - **Average Attendance**: Avg attendance rate across your classes
   - **Average GPA**: Avg GPA in your classes
   - **Improvement Trend**: Whether metrics are improving or declining term-over-term

4. **Class-Level Detail Table** (Table)
   - One row per class section
   - Columns: Course name, period, enrollment, % passed, avg attendance, avg GPA, effectiveness rating
   - **Action**: Identify your strongest and weakest classes for reflection

5. **Student Performance Distribution** (Stacked Bar)
   - Shows breakdown of grades (A, B, C, D, F) across your classes
   - Compares your distribution to school average
   - **What to look for**: If your distribution is skewed (e.g., lots of Ds/Fs), may indicate grading practices or need for differentiation

**Filters Available**:
- **Course**: Select specific course (if you teach multiple preps)
- **Period**: Select specific class period
- **Term**: Current term or compare to previous term

## Section 2: How to Use This Dashboard

### Scenario 1: Identify Your Strongest Class

**Goal**: Understand what's working well so you can replicate it

**Steps**:

1. Look at **"Your Class Pass Rates"** bar chart
2. Identify class with highest pass rate (e.g., "Period 2 Algebra 1: 92% passed")
3. Click on **Class-Level Detail Table**
4. Find Period 2 row, note:
   - Pass rate: 92%
   - Avg attendance: 94%
   - Avg GPA: 3.2
   - Effectiveness rating: "Highly Effective"
5. **Reflect**: What am I doing differently in this class?
   - Classroom management strategies?
   - Relationship-building with students?
   - Instructional approaches (group work, project-based learning)?
   - Assessment practices (formative checks, retakes)?

**Action**: Document what's working and try applying these practices to other classes.

---

### Scenario 2: Diagnose a Struggling Class

**Goal**: Understand why one class is underperforming

**Steps**:

1. Look at **"Your Class Pass Rates"** bar chart
2. Identify class significantly below average (e.g., "Period 5 Algebra 1: 58% passed" vs. school avg 75%)
3. Look at **"Attendance Impact on Pass Rate"** scatter plot
4. Find Period 5 dot on the chart:
   - **If low attendance (e.g., 80%) + low pass rate**: Problem is attendance
     - **Action**: Talk to counselor about attendance barriers for specific students
   - **If high attendance (e.g., 92%) + low pass rate**: Problem may be instructional
     - **Action**: Reflect on instructional practices, consider asking instructional coach for observation

**Possible Factors to Investigate**:
- **Class composition**: Is this class all ELL? All special ed? All 9th graders (less mature)?
- **Time of day**: Is this last period when students are tired?
- **Classroom management**: Is behavior disrupting learning?
- **Pacing**: Are you moving too fast or too slow?
- **Relationships**: Do you have strong rapport with this group?

**Action Plan Options**:
- Request peer observation (ask colleague to observe and give feedback)
- Try different seating arrangement
- Increase formative assessment (exit tickets, quick checks)
- Offer tutoring or office hours for this class
- Adjust pacing or re-teach key concepts

---

### Scenario 3: Set Personal Growth Goals

**Goal**: Use data to set measurable professional goals

**Steps**:

1. Look at **"Your Effectiveness Indicators"** (4 metric cards)
2. Note your current metrics:
   - Pass Rate: 78%
   - Avg Attendance: 89%
   - Avg GPA: 2.4
   - Trend: Stable
3. Compare to school averages:
   - School Pass Rate: 80%
   - School Avg Attendance: 90%
   - School Avg GPA: 2.6
4. Identify gap: Your pass rate is 2 points below school average
5. Set SMART goal:
   - **Specific**: Increase pass rate from 78% to 82%
   - **Measurable**: Track via dashboard monthly
   - **Achievable**: 4 percentage point improvement is realistic
   - **Relevant**: Aligns with school improvement plan
   - **Time-bound**: By end of semester (May 2026)

**Strategies to Achieve Goal**:
- Implement weekly formative assessments to catch struggling students early
- Offer test retakes to allow students to improve grades
- Communicate with parents of failing students monthly
- Attend PD on differentiated instruction

**Track Progress**: Check dashboard monthly to see if pass rate is improving.

---

### Scenario 4: Prepare for Evaluation Meeting with Principal

**Goal**: Bring data to your evaluation conference to support your self-assessment

**Steps**:

1. Open **Dashboard 4: Class Effectiveness Comparison**
2. Take screenshot of **"Your Class Pass Rates"** chart
3. Export **"Class-Level Detail Table"** as CSV
4. In evaluation self-assessment, write:
   - **Strengths**: "My Period 2 class has 92% pass rate, 10 points above school average. I attribute this to strong classroom management and differentiated small group instruction."
   - **Areas for Growth**: "My Period 5 class has 58% pass rate. I've identified attendance as a contributing factor (80% vs. 90% school avg) and have worked with counselor to address barriers. I'm also implementing more frequent formative assessments to catch struggling students earlier."
   - **Goals**: "Increase overall pass rate from 78% to 82% by May 2026 through weekly formative assessments and increased parent communication."

**Bring to Meeting**:
- Dashboard screenshot
- CSV export (detailed class data)
- Examples of student work
- Lesson plans showing differentiation strategies

**Remember**: Dashboard data is **one input**, not the whole story. Your principal will also consider:
- Observations of your teaching
- Student and parent feedback
- Your professional growth activities
- Your collaboration with colleagues

## Section 3: Understanding Your Data

### What Does "Pass Rate" Mean?

**Definition**: Percentage of students in your class with grade C or better at time of data snapshot

**Calculation**:
```
Pass Rate = (# students with A, B, or C) / (Total enrolled students) × 100%
```

**Example**: 
- Class has 30 students
- 5 have A, 8 have B, 10 have C, 4 have D, 3 have F
- Pass rate = (5 + 8 + 10) / 30 = 23 / 30 = **76.7%**

**Important Notes**:
- Pass rate is **snapshot in time** (usually mid-term or end of term)
- Does NOT include students who dropped the class
- May differ from your gradebook if data is not recent (check date range)

---

### What Does "Effectiveness Rating" Mean?

**Rating Categories**:
- **Highly Effective**: Pass rate >85%, attendance >92%, positive trend
- **Effective**: Pass rate 75-85%, attendance 88-92%, stable trend
- **Developing**: Pass rate 65-75%, attendance 85-88%, mixed trend
- **Needs Improvement**: Pass rate <65%, attendance <85%, declining trend

**Important**: Effectiveness rating is **relative to your school's context**, not absolute.
- A 75% pass rate in AP Calculus may be "Highly Effective"
- A 75% pass rate in Algebra 1 Support may be "Developing"
- Context matters: student population, course difficulty, class size

**This is NOT your evaluation rating**. This is a **data point for reflection**.

---

### Why Does Attendance Correlation Matter?

**The Attendance-Achievement Connection**:
- Research shows: **Attendance is the #1 predictor of academic success**
- National average: Every 10% decrease in attendance = ~0.5 GPA point drop

**What the Scatter Plot Tells You**:
- **Expected pattern**: Dots form upward trend (high attendance → high pass rate)
- **Your class above trend line**: You're beating the odds! Students succeeding despite attendance challenges
- **Your class below trend line**: Good attendance but low pass rate → investigate instructional factors

**Common Patterns**:
| Pattern | What It Means | Action |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| High attendance, high pass rate | ✅ Class is thriving | Keep doing what you're doing! |
| High attendance, low pass rate | ⚠️ Instructional concern | Reflect on teaching, seek feedback |
| Low attendance, low pass rate | 📉 Attendance barrier | Work with counselor to improve attendance first |
| Low attendance, high pass rate | 🤔 Unusual - strong students | Praise resilience, still address attendance |

## Section 4: Common Questions

### Q: Why is my pass rate lower than I expected?

**Possible Explanations**:
1. **Data lag**: Dashboard shows data from [X days ago]. Recent grade improvements may not show yet.
2. **Different calculation**: Your gradebook may weight assignments differently than simple A-F count.
3. **Dropped students**: Students who dropped your class mid-term are NOT included in pass rate (they don't count as fail).
4. **Incomplete assignments**: Students with missing work may have F in gradebook but could pass if they complete work.

**Action**: Compare dashboard data to your current gradebook. If discrepancy >5 percentage points, contact analytics team: analytics@district.edu

---

### Q: Can my principal see this data about me?

**Yes**, but with context:
- Principals have access to aggregated class data to monitor school-wide trends
- Your principal may use similar data as **one input** in your evaluation
- However, they also consider: observations, student feedback, your professional development, collaboration, and context (class composition, course difficulty)

**This dashboard is primarily for YOUR reflection**, not evaluation.

---

### Q: How does my data compare to other teachers?

**You cannot see other individual teachers' data** for privacy reasons.

You CAN see:
- **School average** (average across all teachers at your school)
- **District average** (average across all teachers district-wide)

**Why this matters**:
- If your pass rate is below school average, identify what high-performing teachers are doing (ask them!)
- If your pass rate is above school average, share your best practices with colleagues

**Collaboration > Competition**: Use data to learn from each other, not to judge.

---

### Q: What if I teach a challenging population (ELL, SPED, at-risk)?

**Context matters**. A 70% pass rate in:
- **AP Calculus**: Concern (expected: 90%+)
- **Algebra 1 Support (all SPED)**: Success! (expected: 50-60%)

**The dashboard does NOT account for**:
- Student IEP/504 accommodations
- English proficiency levels
- Prior academic history
- Socioeconomic factors

**Action**: In evaluation meetings, **provide context**:
> "My Algebra 1 Support class has 65% pass rate. This class is 100% SPED students, many reading 3+ grade levels below. I've implemented visual aids, hands-on manipulatives, and daily small group instruction. Pass rate has improved 15 percentage points from last term."

**Your principal should consider context**. If they don't, advocate for yourself with data.

---

### Q: How often should I check this dashboard?

**Recommended Frequency**:
- **Mid-term**: Check once to gauge progress, adjust instruction
- **End of term**: Check to reflect on outcomes, set goals for next term
- **Before evaluation meeting**: Prepare data to support self-assessment

**Don't obsess over daily/weekly changes** - focus on long-term trends.

## Section 6: Troubleshooting

### Issue: I can't see any data

**Possible Causes**:
1. You don't have any classes in the system yet (if new teacher)
2. Filter is applied incorrectly (check "Course" and "Period" filters)
3. Data hasn't been loaded yet (if start of year)

**Solution**:
- Click "Reset" on all filters
- If still no data, contact IT: support@district.edu

---

### Issue: My pass rate looks wrong

**Diagnostic Steps**:
1. Check **date range filter** - ensure looking at current term
2. Compare to your gradebook:
   - Count students with A/B/C grades
   - Divide by total enrolled students
   - Does it match dashboard?
3. Check if recent grade changes aren't reflected (data lag)

**If discrepancy is >10 percentage points**:
- Export dashboard data as CSV
- Export gradebook data
- Email both to analytics@district.edu with subject: "Data Discrepancy - [Your Name] - [Course]"

---

### Issue: Dashboard is slow

**Solutions**:
1. **Clear browser cache**: Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac)
2. **Try different browser**: Chrome, Firefox, Safari
3. **Close other tabs**: Dashboard needs memory to load charts
