# Project Intent and Education Analytics Overview

## Original intent
Open Education Analytics (OEA) was created as an open-source data integration and analytics framework for the education sector. The original goal is to empower the education data ecosystem by making it easier to stand up a modern Azure-based lakehouse and to share reusable assets such as pipelines, transformation scripts, notebooks, and dashboards. OEA delivers this through a catalog of modules (single data sources) and packages (multi-source education use cases) so districts, colleges, and partners can accelerate analytics initiatives across K-12 and higher education.

## Types of education and student achievement analysis
The OEA catalogs focus on recurring education data problems and the analysis patterns below, using standardized schemas, pipelines, notebooks, and dashboards.

### Data integration and standardization
- Ingesting Student Information System (SIS), Learning Management System (LMS), assessment, and engagement data.
- Normalizing data with shared schemas (for example, digital engagement and attendance) to make cross-source analysis possible.

### Student attendance and absenteeism risk
- Predictive modeling of chronic absenteeism using attendance records, engagement signals, and known barriers.
- Analysis of attendance trends and targeted intervention opportunities.

### Student attrition and retention risk
- Identifying predictors of student withdrawal or stop-out (credit hours, GPA, course modality).
- Supporting intervention planning with early warning indicators.

### Digital equity of access
- Measuring connectivity, device availability, and network quality (speed, latency).
- Highlighting students or communities with low or no access to digital learning resources.

### Hybrid engagement and digital activity
- Combining in-person attendance with digital activity to gauge overall engagement.
- Comparing engagement across schools, courses, or classes and tracking trends over time.

### Learning analytics and academic progress
- Course-level engagement, resource usage, assignment difficulty, and progress tracking.
- Identifying students needing academic support or tutoring based on engagement and performance signals.

### Student well-being support
- Predictive modeling based on attendance, assessment, health, behavior, safety, demographics, and social-emotional signals.
- Surfacing students who may need well-being assessments or support services.

### Achievement and outcomes analysis
- Linking engagement and participation data to learning outcomes such as assessments, grades, course completion, and graduation measures.
- Evaluating how interventions and programs influence student achievement.

### Responsible AI and model transparency
- Assessing model performance, fairness, and feature importance for predictive use cases.
- Providing dashboards to communicate insights responsibly to educators and leaders.
